## The Four Forces of Progress

### PUSH FORCES (Why Change is Urgent) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**1. Legal Mandate (Extreme Push)**
- EU law - non-compliance = fines
- 2027-2028 deadlines are IMMINENT
- Her company WILL be audited on this data
- Personal liability: "If we get fined, is it my fault?"

**2. Investor Pressure (Extreme Push)**
- ESG funds require CSRD data to invest
- Current investors demanding transparency
- Board asking in every meeting: "Where are we with CSRD?"
- Stock price could suffer if seen as non-compliant

**3. Internal Chaos (Very High Push)**
- Data scattered across 10+ departments
- No central system
- Facilities team uses different metrics than procurement
- Previous year's data doesn't exist in right format
- Manual Excel compilation = weeks of work
- Errors everywhere (wrong units, missing data, duplicates)

- 10+ different ESG standards (GRI, TCFD, SASB) - which applies?
- December 2025 rule changes = "Did the rules change again?!"

**6. Timeline Pressure (Extreme Push)**

- 3 months buffer for errors/corrections
- **She's already behind**

**7. Career Risk (Personal Push)**
- Sarah was hired specifically for this
- If she fails, she's out
- Her professional reputation is on the line

### Core Features (Organized by Job to be Done)

**Job: "I need to collect data from everywhere"**
- Pre-built connectors: SAP, Oracle, Workday, ADP, building management systems
- API access for custom integrations
- CSV/Excel import for systems without APIs
- Mobile app for site-level data entry
- Automated data refresh schedules

**Job: "I need to know what data to collect"**
- ESRS requirement checklist (all 1,000+ data points)
- Materiality wizard: "Based on your industry, here's what's required"
- Data gap dashboard: "You're missing 23 data points"
- Smart suggestions: "Companies like yours collect this data from procurement"

**Job: "I need to ensure data quality"**
- Automated validation rules: "This number seems wrong (3x higher than last year)"
- Duplicate detection
- Unit conversion: automatic kg/tonnes, kWh/MWh conversions
- Completeness scoring: "Your data is 87% complete"
- Audit trail: "Who entered this data and when?"

**Job: "I need to do double materiality assessment"**
- Guided wizard (not 300-page consultant report)
- Pre-populated with industry-specific impacts
- Stakeholder survey tools
- Visual impact/financial materiality matrix
- Documentation generator for audit

**Job: "I need to generate compliant reports"**
- One-click CSRD report generation
- ESRS-compliant structure (automatic)
- Multiple formats: PDF (board), XBRL (regulators), Excel (auditors)
- Version control: "This is v3, here's what changed since v2"
- Track changes mode: "Auditor can see every edit"

**Job: "I need to work with auditors"**
- Auditor portal: Read-only access for Big 4
- Evidence library: Auto-attached source documents
- Comment threads: "Auditor asked question, facilities team responded"
- Audit history: "Here's what was questioned last year"

**Job: "I need to benchmark and improve"**
- Peer comparison: "Your carbon intensity vs. similar companies"
- Trend analysis: "Your water use decreased 12% YoY"
- Improvement recommendations: "Focus on Scope 3 Category 1 (highest impact)"
- Scenario modeling: "If we switch to renewable energy, impact on report?"

**Job: "I need to keep up with regulation changes"**

- Change notifications: 30-day advance notice

# Part 8: Why This Follows JTBD Business Model

## Alignment with "When Coffee and Kale Compete" Principles

### 1. Focus on the Struggle, Not Demographics

‚ùå **Wrong Approach:** "Our target is sustainability managers aged 35-45 at ‚Ç¨500M companies"

‚úÖ **JTBD Approach:** "Our customer is anyone struggling to meet CSRD deadlines and fearing career failure, regardless of age/title"

**How we apply this:**
- Customer discovery asks: "Tell me about the last time you felt stressed about CSRD"
- Marketing speaks to the struggle: "Stop drowning in CSRD spreadsheets"
- Product solves emotional job: Relief from anxiety + confidence

### 2. Four Forces Drive Purchase Decisions

**We designed product to maximize Push/Pull, minimize Anxiety/Inertia:**

| Force | Our Strategy |
|-------|--------------|
| **Push** | Emphasize legal deadline + career risk in marketing |
| **Pull** | Show before/after: "Weeks of work ‚Üí Hours" |
| **Anxiety** | Free trial, case studies, security certs, pilot program |
| **Inertia** | Low because CSRD is new (no "old way" to defend) |

### 3. Competition is Broader Than Category

**We compete with:**
- ‚ùå NOT just other CSRD software
- ‚úÖ Consultants (‚Ç¨500K KPMG engagement)
- ‚úÖ Excel + manual work (free but painful)
- ‚úÖ "Do nothing and hope we don't get audited" (high risk)
- ‚úÖ Hire full-time team (‚Ç¨300K+ in salaries)

**How we position:**
- "Cheaper than consultants, better than Excel, less risk than ignoring it"
- "Software that scales vs. humans who don't"

### 4. Progress Defines Value, Not Outcomes

‚ùå **Wrong:** "We provide CSRD-compliant reports"
‚úÖ **Right:** "We transform you from anxious/behind-schedule ‚Üí confident/on-track"

**How we sell this:**
- Sales demo focuses on Sarah's journey: "Here's Sarah before... here's Sarah after"
- Case studies talk about feelings: "I finally slept at night" not "We generated a report"
- Product celebrates progress: "üéâ Your report is 87% complete!" (not just data)

### 5. Hire Products to Make Progress

**Sarah hires our product to:**
- Fire: Stressful Excel chaos, expensive consultants, career risk
- Hire: Automated confidence, time savings, professional reputation

**How we reinforce hiring:**
- Onboarding says: "You made the right decision, here's how we'll help you win"
- Customer success measures progress: "You're 3 weeks ahead of schedule"
- Testimonials focus on transformation: "Clarity CSRD saved my job"

### 6. Build for "Job Performers" Not "Job Executors"

**Job Performer:** Person experiencing the struggle (Sarah, sustainability manager)
**Job Executor:** Person technically doing task (data entry person)

**We optimize for performer (decision maker), not executor:**
- UI designed for Sarah (non-technical, stressed, needs clarity)
- NOT designed for IT department (they don't feel the pain)
- Pricing authority: CFO/CSO (performers), not IT (executors)
