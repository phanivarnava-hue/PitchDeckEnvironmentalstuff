# Danish Heat-Link {.cover}

## Learning from Denmark to Accelerate Dutch District Heating

# The Problem

### The Netherlands faces a heating transition crisis:

- **Gas Phase-Out**: All homes must be gas-free by 2050, 7 million connections to convert
- **District Heating Solution**: Shared heat grids are the best alternative, but rollout is slow
- **Social Resistance**: Residents fear forced conversion, distrust costs, lack information
- **Planning Paralysis**: Municipalities don't know which neighborhoods are ready to switch
- **Danish Success**: Denmark has 60%+ district heating penetration - we have only 5%
- **Lost Learning**: Proven Danish model of transparency and community buy-in not applied

**Result**: Gas phase-out deadlines at risk, billions wasted on wrong neighborhoods first

# How It Works

### For Residents:
1. **Explore**: View interactive map of their neighborhood
2. **Learn**: See transparent info on district heating costs vs. current gas bills
3. **Calculate**: Personal savings calculator (monthly costs, carbon reduction)
4. **Pin**: Mark their house as "interested" with one click
5. **Track Progress**: See real-time % of street committed
6. **Share**: Encourage neighbors to join via social sharing
7. **Get Updates**: Notified when threshold reached and municipality takes action

### For Municipalities:
1. **Dashboard**: See all neighborhoods and their readiness levels
2. **Heat Maps**: Visual representation of interest concentration
3. **Auto Reports**: When neighborhood hits 70%, system generates viability study
4. **Resident Data**: Understand concerns, questions, and preferences
5. **Prioritization**: Focus infrastructure investment where social buy-in exists
6. **Communication**: Direct messaging to interested residents
7. **Danish Comparison**: Benchmark against similar Danish neighborhoods

### For National Government:
1. **Monitor**: Track district heating readiness across Netherlands
2. **Identify**: Which regions are ready to move fast vs. need more education
3. **Allocate**: Direct subsidies to neighborhoods with proven demand
4. **Learn**: Which messaging and transparency works best
5. **Track**: Progress toward 2030 and 2050 gas-free targets
6. **Export**: Apply lessons to other policy transitions

# The Danish Success Story

### Why Denmark Leads:
- **Transparency First**: All costs and savings publicly available before commitment
- **Community Choice**: Neighborhoods vote to join, not forced
- **Price Regulation**: Government ensures fair, stable pricing
- **Consumer Protection**: Strong guarantees reduce fear
- **Gradual Rollout**: Started in 1970s, built trust over time
- **Public Ownership**: Many heat grids municipally owned = trust

### What Netherlands Can Learn:
- **Transparency Reduces Resistance**: Danish openness model key to acceptance
- **Let Communities Lead**: Crowdsourcing readiness vs. top-down mandates
- **Economics Matter**: Show real savings, not just climate benefits
- **Local Ownership**: Consider municipal vs. corporate ownership models
- **Long-Term View**: Danish 50-year vision vs. Dutch rushed 2050 deadline
